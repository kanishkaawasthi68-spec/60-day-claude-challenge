# Day 23 – Customer & MVP Blueprint

## Overview

Today, I worked on defining the customer strategy and MVP roadmap for the AgriTech Intelligence Platform. The focus was on understanding the target users, identifying their biggest pain points, validating the core problem, and determining the minimum viable product (MVP) that should be built first.

## What Was Done

### Customer Research

* Defined the Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).
* Created detailed farmer personas.
* Identified customer goals, frustrations, motivations, and buying behavior.
* Analyzed how farmers currently solve their problems.

### Pain Point Analysis

* Identified the top challenges faced by farmers.
* Studied issues related to soil health, crop selection, market access, pest management, and weather uncertainty.
* Prioritized pain points based on severity and frequency.

### Customer Journey Mapping

* Mapped the complete farmer journey from awareness to retention.
* Defined user actions, emotions, and product touchpoints at each stage.
* Identified key moments that create trust and engagement.

### MVP Planning

* Defined the first version of the product.
* Prioritized features using the MoSCoW framework.
* Selected Soil Advisory, Crop Recommendation, and Live Mandi Price Feed as the core MVP features.
* Deferred advanced features such as IoT monitoring, international marketplace, and financing solutions to future phases.

### Business & Validation Strategy

* Created pricing hypotheses for free and premium plans.
* Identified major product and market risks.
* Defined success metrics for the MVP.
* Prepared a 30-day validation and execution roadmap.

## Outcome

Successfully created a complete Customer & MVP Blueprint for the AgriTech Intelligence Platform, providing a clear roadmap for customer validation, product development, feature prioritization, and market entry strategy.
