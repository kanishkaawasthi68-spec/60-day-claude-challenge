# Day 21 - Digital Privacy Dashboard

## Objective

Built an interactive Digital Privacy Dashboard using Claude AI and analyzed a sample digital footprint dataset.

## Dataset Used

Services:

* Instagram
* Snapchat
* TikTok
* YouTube
* Discord
* WhatsApp
* iMessage
* Spotify
* Roblox
* PUBG Mobile
* Amazon
* Meesho
* Google Search
* Google Pay
* Google Photos

## Dashboard Features

* Digital Footprint Score
* Privacy Score
* Exposure Heatmap
* Company Exposure Ranking
* Data Collection Matrix
* Risk Radar
* Digital Twin Profile
* Most Valuable Data Assets
* Privacy Improvement Simulator
* Final Verdict

## Key Findings

* Digital Footprint Score: 74/100 (Significant Exposure)
* Privacy Score: 34/100 (Fair Privacy Posture)
* Google and Meta contribute the highest exposure levels.
* Behavioral tracking is the largest privacy risk.
* Search history, social activity, and shopping behavior are the most valuable data assets.

## Privacy Risks Identified

1. Extensive cross-platform tracking.
2. High behavioral profiling through social media usage.
3. Large search and browsing data collection.
4. Potential location and activity tracking.
5. Strong ecosystem concentration around Google services.

## Recommended Improvements

* Review Google Account privacy settings.
* Disable unnecessary activity tracking.
* Limit personalized advertising.
* Audit social media privacy permissions.
* Regularly clear watch/search history.
* Use privacy-focused browsers and search engines.

## Learnings

* Learned how digital footprints are analyzed.
* Understood privacy scoring methodologies.
* Explored data collection practices of major platforms.
* Learned how exposure rankings and risk radar visualizations work.
* Practiced generating advanced HTML dashboards using AI.
